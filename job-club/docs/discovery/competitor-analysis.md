# Competitor Analysis

## Competitor 1: Major League Hacking (MLH)

### Overview
- Student hackathon league
- Fellowship programs
- Large community

### What They Do Well
- Strong brand recognition
- Regular events (hackathons)
- Fellowship provides real experience
- Large Discord community
- Professional website

### Weaknesses
- Focused on hackathons (not career building)
- Less personalized guidance
- No portfolio building focus
- Competitive entry (fellowship)

### What We Can Learn
- Community building through Discord
- Regular events create engagement
- Clear value proposition
- Professional visual identity

---

## Competitor 2: Replit Community

### Overview
- Online coding platform
- Built-in community features
- Project sharing

### What They Do Well
- Low barrier to entry
- Instant coding environment
- Project sharing/remixing
- Gamification (Cycles)
- Real-time collaboration

### Weaknesses
- Not focused on AI careers
- No structured career guidance
- Platform-dependent skills
- Less professional development focus

### What We Can Learn
- Make it easy to start
- Show projects/portfolios publicly
- Community interaction features
- Gamification for motivation

---

## Competitor 3: University Career Centers

### Overview
- NJIT Career Development Services
- Traditional career support

### What They Do Well
- Official university support
- Resume reviews
- Job fairs
- Employer connections

### Weaknesses
- Generic (not AI-focused)
- Slow response times
- Limited personalization
- Not tech-native experience
- No community aspect

### What We Can Learn
- Connect with official resources
- Resume/portfolio guidance
- Employer networking
